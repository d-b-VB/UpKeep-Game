2-player is the current head-to-head map. 1-player starts on 19 tiles and reveals more world as units approach.

How to play
  • Grow economy by owning productive tiles (top-down resources in the side panel are your priority chain).
  • Train units on owned settlements/fortifications, then move/attack to claim territory.
  • Upgrade terrain and settlements to unlock higher-tier units and resources.
  • Avoid shortages: negative available resources can disband units/lose structures at end turn.
  • Closed terrain blocks some movement/attacks; use forts/settlements and unit types tactically.

Unit guide: abilities, upkeep, training, lore
Worker lines
Worker/Axman/Laborer/Surveyor/Architect shape the map and economy. In-world: they are settlers, foremen, and survey corps building frontier infrastructure while moving with the army.
Infantry
Spearman/Swordsman/Pikeman/Sergeant hold lanes and pressure closed terrain. Spearmen can stabilize farms in lore, representing mixed militia-farming duties while stationed.
Archers
Hunter/Longbow/Crossbow/Barrage Captain project force before melee. Lore: these are trained skirmish and siege lines operating from prepared positions and supply hubs.
Cavalry
Horseman/Lancer/Cavalry Archer/Royal Knight handle mobility and breakthroughs. Lore: mounted nobles and outriders exploit roads/fields and punish exposed fronts.
Training sources
Settlements train the civil + line troops; great houses train support specialists; forts train defensive elites. Higher tiers reflect stronger local institutions and logistics.
Upkeep logic
Upkeep represents food, draft animals, and command overhead. Free-upkeep zones (e.g., forts) model depots/quartermasters sustaining garrisons in place.
